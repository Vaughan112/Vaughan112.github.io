Family Funeral Potatoes Recipe


History

Funeral potatoes are a cheesy potato casserole with a history deeply intertwined with the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS), particularly in Utah. They became popular in the mid-20th century, likely from the LDS Relief Society, a women's organization that served meals at funeral luncheons. The dish's reliance on shelf-stable, inexpensive convenience foods made it easy to prepare in large quantities for community gatherings, and its ingredients could be stored in pantry stockpiles promoted by the Church

Description

Its a cheesy potato casserole with chips on top. Normally called Funeral Potatoes.


Ingredients

  • 2lb bag Ore-Ida hash brown potatoes
  • 1 can Cream of Chicken Soup
  • 1 cup diced onion or less, depending on your taste
  • 16 oz. carton sour cream
  • 1 stick margarine
  • 8 oz. sharp shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt & Pepper to taste
  • 1 cup crushed potato chips

Directions

  1. Remove potatoes from freezer about 1/2 hour before making dish
  2. Mix all ingredients other than potato chips together
  3. Put into 9 x 13 glass baking dish
  4. Add potato chips to the top
  5. Bake at 375 for 1 hour
  6. For best flavor, make it refrigerate day or night before baking
